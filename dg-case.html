SPD: Dolce & Gabbana

  • Role:UI/UX Designer
  • Context: Master's Project
  • Date:December 2016

Project Overview

Design an in-store mobile registration application for Dolce & Gabbana employees that allows them to capture client’s personal information. The application experience should be consistent with the luxury experience of the retail store.

Analysis & Overview

Redundant Fields

Currently there are too many repeating fields which overly complicates the registration process.

Unnecessary Questions

Users must provide plenty of personal information, most of which is unnecessary, to simply receive emails.

Ultimately, these pain points presented an overly complicated process and an experience that was the exact opposite of what a customer would receive in Dolce & Gabbana retail stores.

Client Personas

To develop a deeper understanding of potential clients along with their goals and motivations for interacting with the in-store application, character personas were developed.

Stephanie Anderson

Stephanie is a 26 year old Fashion blogger from Birmingham, UK. Stephanie has a very strong social media presence and is constantly generating new content to share with her followers. She has a large appetite for purchasing new things and is willing to spend large amounts of money on quality items.

Before entering D&G, Stephanie does her research beforehand and knows exactly what she wants to purchase.

Katherine Langston

Katherine is a 41 year old Pharmacist living in Vancouver,Canada. Katherine spends most of her time in her office and largely relies upon her smartphone and tablet for the majority of her communications.

While Katherine doesn’t usually purchase items online, she does the majority of her research online rather than in-store. However, when making purchases, Katherine prefers to go to the store to view the products in person, as long as it doesn’t take too much of her time.

Design Goals

Taking these pain points and personas into account, the following design goals were created and acted as the foundations of the in-store application.

  • 1

    Develop an incentive for providing information

  • 2

    Reduce amount of time and information required from clients

  • 3

    Make process simple and enjoyable for employees

Customer Journey Flow

Prior to the design phase, a customer journey flow was created to develop a full understanding and visualisation behind all of the components of the system.

Register or Find Client

From the main screen, employees can quickly register new clients or search for existing ones.

Personal Information

Only the most necessary and important client information is required. Clients can now also choose their preferred method of contact.

Purpose of Contact

Rather than receiving generic and unwanted information, clients can now choose the exact reasons they would like to be contacted for; ultimately allowing for a more customised and personal experience.

Payment Information

Clients now have the ability to save their payment information to allow for simple and efficient purchases in the future. If clients don’t want to do so, they can skip this option and complete their registration.

Payment Details

If clients choose to save a payment option to their account, employees can quickly scan card information using the phone camera.

Payment Details (Manual)

Employees also have the option to manually add payment information to a client’s account.

Confirmation

Before completing the registration process and becoming part of the D&G family, clients are asked to confirm their actions one last time.
